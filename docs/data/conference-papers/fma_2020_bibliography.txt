**Core Paper on Property-Based Floodplain Management**

*   **Title:** Purchase or Move? Lessons Learnt from Historic Property-Based Floodplain Management Schemes [1], [2]
*   **Authors:** Alana Mosely [3], [1]
*   **Year Published:** 2020 [4]
*   **Key Topic:** This paper directly addresses the core context by analyzing different **scheme types** such as voluntary and compulsory house purchases, land-swaps, and house resilience/wet-proofing schemes [5]. The study details the **eligibility criteria** used to identify qualifying properties (such as repetitive losses or location in high hazard areas) [6], [7]. It also explores the social challenges involved in these schemes, identifying key **success factors** (e.g., sound community engagement, giving homeowners options, strong local leadership) and **lessons learnt** (e.g., providing a useful purpose for vacated land, managing rising house prices, and recognizing that the elderly find it more difficult to relocate) from historic global schemes [2], [6], [8], [9].
