# FMA 2024 Conference: Floods in a Changing Climate

## Overview

The Floodplain Management Australia (FMA) 2024 conference focused on *Floods in a Changing Climate*, bringing together practitioners, researchers, and policymakers across five major themes. This node captures the key concepts and emerging issues presented at the conference.

---

## Conference Themes

### [Climate Change & Future Conditions](climate-change.md)

- **Updating National Guidelines** — How ARR and ANCOLD guidance is evolving to incorporate climate change
- **Translating Science to Policy** — Bridging the gap between climate projections and regulatory frameworks
- **Impacts on Planning Levels** — Reassessing AEP-based design standards under non-stationary conditions
- **Tomorrow's Floodplain** — Forward-looking approaches to floodplain mapping and management

### [Technical Modelling & Flood Estimation](technical-modelling.md)

- **Reconciling Discrepancies** — Addressing differences between deterministic and probabilistic methods
- **Updating IFD Data** — Bureau of Meteorology's updated Intensity-Frequency-Duration relationships
- **Extending Short Records** — Regional methods, historical data, and paleoflood information
- **Implications of Changing FFA** — How flood frequency analysis methods are adapting to climate variability

### [Forecasting, Monitoring & Emergency Response](forecasting-emergency.md)

- **Real-Time Flash Flood Forecasting** — Advances in short-term warning systems
- **Distributed Sensor Networks** — IoT and remote sensing for catchment monitoring
- **Resilient Infrastructure** — Designing for forecast-based emergency management
- **Compound Hazards** — Concurrent flooding, storm surge, and extreme rainfall events

### [Community Engagement & Risk Communication](community-engagement.md)

- **Bridging the Language Gap** — Communicating probability and uncertainty to non-technical audiences
- **Indigenous Knowledge & Co-design** — Incorporating First Nations perspectives in flood risk management
- **Managing Customer Perceptions** — Insurance industry and community understanding of flood risk
- **Post-Flood Support** — Recovery frameworks and community resilience building
- **STEM Education** — Building flood literacy in schools and communities

### [Urban Planning, Infrastructure & Policy Management](urban-planning.md)

- **Urban Densification** — Impacts of increased imperviousness and infill development on flood risk
- **Impacts on Open Spaces** — Flood storage, detention basins, and blue-green infrastructure
- **Rural Floodplain Management** — Agricultural impacts and regional flood planning
- **Insurance & Hydrology Assessments** — The role of hydrologists in insurance pricing and coverage
- **International Perspectives** — Lessons from overseas flood management approaches

---

## Key Takeaways

The conference highlighted several unresolved tensions in Australian flood practice:

- The gap between climate science (non-stationarity) and regulatory frameworks (fixed AEP standards)
- The need for real-time data integration in operational flood forecasting
- The challenge of communicating probabilistic risk to communities and decision-makers
- The growing importance of urban flood management as cities densify
